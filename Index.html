Galvanized steel is steel that has been coated with zinc to protect it from corrosion. The zinc coating is applied through a process called galvanization, which can be done by hot-dipping, electroplating, or annealing. Galvanized steel is used in many industries, including construction, aerospace, automotive, and oil and gas.

Stainless steel, also known as inox, corrosion-resistant steel (CRES), and rustless steel, is an alloy of iron that is resistant to rusting and corrosion. It contains iron with chromium and other elements such as molybdenum, carbon, nickel and nitrogen depending on its specific use and cost.
